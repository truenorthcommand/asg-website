# What Property Managers Need to Know About Compliance & Safety Standards

**Published:** March 2026  
**Category:** Compliance | Legal  
**Reading Time:** 8 minutes

## The Compliance Minefield

If you manage rental properties in the UK, you're operating in one of the most heavily regulated sectors. Miss a deadline or overlook a requirement, and you're facing fines, legal action, and potential prosecution.

The problem? Compliance requirements are scattered across multiple acts, regulations, and standards. Most property managers don't know what they don't know until it's too late.

This guide covers the essential compliance checks every property manager must know about. It's not exhaustive—but it covers 95% of what will get you into trouble.

## The Big Four: Mandatory Checks

These four compliance requirements are non-negotiable. They're legally required, they have specific deadlines, and they carry significant fines if missed.

### 1. Gas Safety Check (Annual)

**Legal Requirement:** Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998

**What it is:** A qualified Gas Safe engineer inspects all gas appliances (boiler, cooker, fire, heater) and gas pipework.

**Frequency:** Once every 12 months

**Cost:** £50–£100

**Deadline:** Must be completed within 12 months of the previous check. Record must be provided to tenant within 30 days of check.

**Penalty for non-compliance:** £6,000 fine or prosecution (potential jail time in serious cases)

**What the engineer checks:**
- Boiler efficiency and safety
- Cooker ignition and burner function
- Gas pipe integrity
- Ventilation and flue safety
- Carbon monoxide levels

**Pro tip:** Schedule this in September/October before winter. If your boiler fails in November, you're in breach if you haven't had the annual check.

### 2. Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) – Every 5 Years

**Legal Requirement:** Electrical Safety Standards in the Private Rented Sector (England) Regulations 2020

**What it is:** A qualified electrician inspects all electrical installations, wiring, sockets, and appliances for safety hazards.

**Frequency:** Every 5 years (or more frequently if issues are found)

**Cost:** £150–£250

**Deadline:** Must be completed before a new tenancy begins. Existing tenancies must be compliant by April 2021 (if you haven't done this, you're already in breach).

**Penalty for non-compliance:** £5,000+ fine

**What the electrician checks:**
- Wiring condition and insulation
- Socket and switch safety
- Earth bonding
- RCD (Residual Current Device) protection
- Appliance safety (if included in scope)

**Pro tip:** Get an EICR done now if you haven't had one since 2020. It's the most commonly missed requirement.

### 3. Gas Safety Check for Appliances (Annual)

**Legal Requirement:** Landlord and Tenant Act 1985

**What it is:** A Gas Safe engineer certifies that all gas appliances are safe to use.

**Frequency:** Once every 12 months

**Cost:** £50–£100 (often combined with boiler service)

**Deadline:** Must be done before the tenancy starts and annually thereafter. Tenant must receive a copy within 30 days.

**Penalty for non-compliance:** £6,000 fine or prosecution

**Note:** This is separate from the boiler service. A boiler service checks efficiency; a gas safety check confirms safety.

### 4. Legionella Risk Assessment (As Needed)

**Legal Requirement:** Control of Legionella Bacteria in Water Regulations 2010

**What it is:** An assessment of water systems (boiler, hot water tank, shower, etc.) for legionella bacteria risk.

**Frequency:** At least every 2 years (more often if high-risk factors exist)

**Cost:** £150–£300

**Deadline:** Must be completed before a new tenancy and reviewed regularly.

**Penalty for non-compliance:** £5,000–£20,000 fine

**When you need this:** If your property has a complex water system, a hot tub, or multiple showers, you're at higher risk.

**Pro tip:** Most standard rental properties have low legionella risk. A simple assessment (not a full remedial plan) usually suffices.

## The Secondary Requirements: Important But Less Obvious

### Damp & Mold (Duty to Maintain Habitable Standard)

**Legal Requirement:** Landlord and Tenant Act 1985, Housing Act 2004

**What it is:** You must maintain the property in a state fit for human habitation. This includes preventing damp and mold.

**What this means:**
- Regular gutter cleaning (prevents water ingress)
- Adequate ventilation (prevents condensation)
- Prompt repair of leaks (prevents damp)
- Inspection for mold growth

**Penalty for non-compliance:** Tenant can claim compensation (£500–£5,000+), local authority enforcement, or prosecution

**Pro tip:** Take photos of the property condition at the start of each tenancy. If mold appears, document whether it's due to poor maintenance (your fault) or tenant behavior (their fault).

### Fire Safety (HMOs Only)

**Legal Requirement:** Fire Safety Order 2005

**What it is:** If you manage a House in Multiple Occupation (HMO)—a property with 3+ unrelated occupants—you must ensure fire safety measures are in place.

**Requirements:**
- Fire risk assessment
- Smoke alarms on every floor
- Emergency lighting
- Fire extinguishers
- Clear escape routes

**Frequency:** Fire risk assessment every 1–3 years

**Cost:** £300–£800 for assessment; £500–£2,000 for remedial work

**Penalty for non-compliance:** £5,000–£20,000 fine or prosecution

**Pro tip:** If you're unsure whether your property is an HMO, check the definition. It's stricter than you think.

### Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)

**Legal Requirement:** Energy Performance of Buildings Regulations 2012

**What it is:** A certificate showing the property's energy efficiency rating (A–G, with A being most efficient).

**Frequency:** Required before marketing a property and before a tenancy starts. Valid for 10 years.

**Cost:** £50–£120

**Deadline:** Must be provided to prospective tenants before they view the property.

**Penalty for non-compliance:** £500–£5,000 fine

**Pro tip:** An EPC is often the first thing prospective tenants see. A low rating (E–G) can deter applicants. Consider energy improvements (insulation, boiler upgrade) to improve the rating.

### Asbestos Survey (If Property Built Before 2000)

**Legal Requirement:** Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012

**What it is:** A survey to identify asbestos-containing materials in the property.

**Frequency:** Once, unless major work is planned

**Cost:** £200–£500

**Deadline:** Should be completed before renovation or maintenance work begins

**Penalty for non-compliance:** £5,000–£20,000 fine if asbestos is disturbed without proper handling

**Pro tip:** If asbestos is found, don't panic. It's safe if left undisturbed. Only licensed contractors can remove it.

## The Compliance Calendar: What to Do When

### January
- Review all compliance records from the previous year
- Schedule gas safety checks for spring/summer
- Plan electrical inspections (if due in the next 5 years)

### February–March
- Complete gas safety checks (before heating season ends)
- Inspect gutters and drainage
- Check for damp or mold

### April–May
- Schedule electrical inspections (if due)
- Review fire safety measures (if HMO)
- Plan any major maintenance work

### June–August
- Complete routine maintenance
- Inspect roof and external walls
- Clean gutters (second time this year)

### September–October
- Schedule boiler service and gas safety check (before winter)
- Inspect heating system
- Prepare property for winter

### November–December
- Verify all gas and electrical certifications are current
- Prepare compliance records for the next year
- Plan spring maintenance

## Red Flags: When You're Out of Compliance

If any of these apply to you, you're likely in breach:

- **No gas safety certificate in the last 12 months**
- **No EICR in the last 5 years (or since 2020)**
- **Visible damp or mold in the property**
- **Boiler hasn't been serviced in the last 12 months**
- **Tenant hasn't received a copy of the gas safety certificate**
- **Property is an HMO without a fire risk assessment**
- **No EPC on file**

If any of these apply, contact a qualified professional immediately. The cost of fixing it now is far less than the cost of a fine or legal action.
