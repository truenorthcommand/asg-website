# Emergency Response Case Study: How We Fixed a Catastrophic Leak in 90 Minutes

**Published:** March 2026  
**Category:** Case Study | Emergency Response  
**Reading Time:** 5 minutes

## The Call: 11:47 PM on a Tuesday

A property manager in Maidstone called us at 11:47 PM on a Tuesday night. His words: "Water is pouring from the ceiling. The tenant is panicking. I don't know what to do."

The property was a 3-bedroom semi-detached rental. The tenant had noticed water dripping from the upstairs ceiling around 11 PM. By the time the property manager arrived, the drip had become a stream. Water was pooling on the landing floor.

The tenant was threatening to leave. The property manager was considering an emergency hotel booking. The cost was spiraling.

This is the moment that separates reactive property management from proactive. And it's the moment ASG's 24/7 response makes a difference.

## The Response: Within 4 Hours

We arrived at 12:23 AM—36 minutes after the call. Here's what we did:

### Step 1: Immediate Containment (5 minutes)

First priority: stop the water. We located the main water shut-off valve and turned it off. The flow stopped immediately.

We then placed buckets and towels under the leak to catch any remaining water and prevent further damage to the ceiling and floor below.

**Cost of this step:** £0 (emergency response fee covers it)  
**Value:** Prevented an additional £2,000+ in water damage

### Step 2: Diagnosis (15 minutes)

We accessed the loft above the leak. The source was immediately obvious: a pipe joint had corroded and fractured. Water had been dripping for some time before the tenant noticed—the loft insulation was saturated.

The pipe was copper, approximately 15mm diameter, and the fracture was at a joint (the weakest point). This is a common failure point in older properties, especially in areas with hard water.

**Key finding:** The pipe had been under pressure for months. The tenant's shower use that evening had finally pushed it past the breaking point.

### Step 3: Temporary Repair (20 minutes)

We couldn't replace the entire pipe at midnight. Instead, we used a compression fitting and repair clamp to seal the fracture. This is a temporary fix—designed to hold for 24–48 hours while a permanent repair is planned.

We also isolated the section of pipe to prevent future pressure buildup.

**Cost of temporary repair:** £85 (parts and labor)  
**Timeline:** 20 minutes  
**Reliability:** 99% effective for short-term use

### Step 4: Restoration (30 minutes)

We turned the water back on and tested the repair. No leaks. We then:
- Removed the buckets and towels
- Dried the ceiling and floor with towels and a portable dehumidifier
- Documented the damage with photos
- Provided the property manager with a written report

**Cost:** Included in emergency response fee

### Step 5: Next Steps (Communicated at 1:15 AM)

We explained to the property manager that:
1. The temporary repair is safe but not permanent
2. The pipe needs to be replaced within 48 hours
3. The loft insulation is wet and needs to dry (or be replaced)
4. We can schedule the permanent repair for the following evening

**Total time on-site:** 90 minutes  
**Total emergency response cost:** £245 (call-out fee + temporary repair parts)

## The Outcome: Crisis Averted

By 1:30 AM, the water had stopped, the property was secure, and the tenant was reassured. No emergency hotel booking was needed. The tenant stayed in the property.

The permanent repair was completed the following evening (Wednesday, 6 PM). We replaced 3 meters of corroded pipe with new copper pipe and compression fittings. Cost: £380.

The wet loft insulation was dried using a commercial dehumidifier over 48 hours. No replacement was needed.

**Total cost for emergency response + permanent repair:** £625

## Why This Matters: The Bigger Picture

This case study illustrates three critical points:

### 1. 24/7 Response Prevents Cascading Damage

Water damage doesn't stop at the source. It spreads. It seeps. It causes mold. Every hour a leak goes unaddressed, the damage multiplies.

Our 90-minute response prevented hours of additional damage. The difference between arriving at midnight and arriving at 8 AM is thousands of pounds.

### 2. Temporary Fixes Buy Time for Permanent Solutions

We didn't have to replace the entire pipe at midnight. A temporary repair held the situation stable until we could do a proper job during normal working hours.

This is practical problem-solving. Emergency doesn't mean expensive. It means fast and effective.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: this leak could have been prevented.

An annual plumbing inspection would have identified the corroded pipe joint. A replacement would have cost £300–£400 and taken 2–3 hours during the day.

Instead, the property manager paid £625 for an emergency response + permanent repair, plus the stress of a midnight crisis.

## What We Learned (And What You Should Know)

### For Property Managers:
- Corrosion in copper pipes is common in hard water areas (Kent is one of them)
- Pipe joints are the weakest point—they fail first
- Annual plumbing inspections catch these issues before they become emergencies
- 24/7 response availability is essential—not optional

### For Landlords:
