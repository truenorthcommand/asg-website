# ASG Blog Brand Voice Guidelines

## Core Principles

**Precision First:** Every claim must be verifiable. No vague language, no marketing fluff. ASG is about measurable outcomes, not aspirations.

**Outcome-Focused:** Always connect advice to business impact (speed, margin, capacity, control, clarity). Show the "why" and the "what's in it for them."

**Practical & Actionable:** Readers should walk away with specific steps they can take immediately. Avoid theoretical discussions.

**Authority Through Expertise:** Write as someone who has solved these problems 100 times. Reference real-world scenarios, edge cases, and lessons learned.

**No Corporate Speak:** Avoid buzzwords, jargon, and motivational language. Use clear, direct English. Contractions are fine.

## Tone

- **Professional but conversational** — not stiff, not casual
- **Confident** — you know what you're talking about
- **Honest** — acknowledge limitations, trade-offs, and when to call a professional
- **Direct** — get to the point quickly

## Content Structure

### Maintenance Tips (Monday Posts)

**Format:**
1. **Hook (1-2 sentences):** Why this matters right now (seasonal, common problem, cost-saving opportunity)
2. **Problem Statement (1 paragraph):** What happens if you ignore this?
3. **5 Actionable Steps (numbered list with brief explanations)**
4. **Timeline (1 sentence):** When to do this (e.g., "Do this every spring before the rainy season")
5. **Cost/Benefit (1 paragraph):** Rough cost estimate + savings/risk avoided
6. **When to Call a Professional (1 paragraph):** Red flags that require expert help
7. **CTA:** "Need help? Contact ASG for a free assessment"

**Tone:** Educational, practical, slightly urgent (but not alarmist)

**Keywords:** Target long-tail keywords (e.g., "winter plumbing maintenance checklist," "how to prevent roof leaks")

**Example Opening:**
"Spring is here, and your gutters are probably clogged with debris from winter. A blocked gutter costs you nothing to fix today—or thousands in water damage next month. Here's what to do."

### Case Studies (Wednesday Posts)

**Format:**
1. **Headline:** "[Property Type] Refurbishment: [Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
2. **The Challenge (1-2 paragraphs):** What was the problem? Why was it urgent?
3. **The Solution (2-3 paragraphs):** What did ASG do? Why that approach?
4. **The Results (1 paragraph with metrics):** Specific numbers: cost saved, time reduced, capacity gained, quality improved
5. **Key Lesson (1 paragraph):** What's the takeaway for other property owners?
6. **CTA:** "Have a similar property? Let's talk"

**Tone:** Confident, results-driven, specific

**Keywords:** Property type + location + service (e.g., "Kent commercial property refurbishment," "emergency plumbing response")

**Example:**
"A 50-unit residential complex in Ashford faced a critical electrical issue that threatened to shut down the building. ASG mobilized a 24/7 emergency team, diagnosed the fault in 2 hours, and had power restored by dawn. Here's how we did it."

### Emergency Response Insights (Friday Posts)

**Format:**
1. **Scenario (1-2 sentences):** A real emergency situation
2. **First 30 Minutes (1 paragraph):** What should the property owner do?
3. **What ASG Does (2 paragraphs):** Our protocol, response time, diagnostic approach
4. **Common Mistakes (1 paragraph):** What NOT to do
5. **Prevention (1 paragraph):** How to reduce the risk of this happening again
6. **CTA:** "24/7 Emergency Line: 01233 564666"

**Tone:** Calm, authoritative, reassuring

**Keywords:** "Emergency [service]," "24/7 response," "[service] emergency guide"

**Example:**
"It's 2 AM on a Sunday. Your tenant calls: water is pouring from the ceiling. Here's exactly what you should do in the first 30 minutes—and what we'll do when we arrive."

## Writing Rules

### Do's

- ✅ Use short sentences and paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- ✅ Use numbered lists and bullet points for clarity
- ✅ Include specific numbers (costs, timeframes, percentages)
- ✅ Reference seasonal or time-sensitive triggers
- ✅ Link to related ASG services (e.g., "See our Plumbing & Heating services")
- ✅ End with a clear CTA (contact form, phone number, or service link)
- ✅ Use H2 and H3 subheadings liberally
- ✅ Write in active voice ("We fixed the issue" not "The issue was fixed")

### Don'ts

- ❌ Don't use "we believe," "we think," or "in our opinion" — state facts
- ❌ Don't use ALL CAPS for emphasis (use bold or italics instead)
- ❌ Don't write paragraphs longer than 4 sentences
- ❌ Don't include irrelevant company history or mission statements
- ❌ Don't use metaphors or analogies (be literal)
- ❌ Don't say "contact us for more information" — be specific about what they'll get
- ❌ Don't write about topics outside ASG's service areas

### SEO Guidelines

- **Title (H1):** 50-60 characters, include primary keyword, action-oriented
- **Meta Description:** 155-160 characters, include primary keyword, include benefit
- **Slug:** lowercase, hyphens, 3-5 words (e.g., "winter-plumbing-maintenance-tips")
- **Excerpt:** 100-120 characters, summarize the main benefit
- **Internal Links:** 2-3 links to relevant ASG service pages or other blog posts
- **Read Time:** Aim for 5-7 minutes (800-1200 words)

## AI Generation Prompts

When generating blog posts, use these prompts:

**Maintenance Tips:**
"Write a maintenance tip blog post for ASG, a UK property maintenance company. Topic: [seasonal maintenance task]. Audience: UK property owners and landlords. Tone: practical, confident, outcome-focused. Structure: hook, problem, 5 actionable steps, timeline, cost/benefit, when to call a pro, CTA. Length: 800-1000 words. Include specific numbers and timelines. No fluff, no corporate speak."

**Case Studies:**
"Write a case study blog post for ASG. Property type: [type]. Challenge: [problem]. Solution: [what ASG did]. Results: [specific metrics]. Tone: confident, results-driven. Structure: headline, challenge, solution, results with metrics, key lesson, CTA. Length: 800-1000 words. Make it specific and credible."

**Emergency Response:**
"Write an emergency response insight blog post for ASG. Scenario: [emergency type]. Tone: calm, authoritative, reassuring. Structure: scenario, first 30 minutes, what ASG does, common mistakes, prevention, CTA. Length: 600-800 words. Be specific about protocols and response times."

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Title is action-oriented and includes primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description is 155-160 characters and includes benefit
- [ ] Slug is lowercase with hyphens
- [ ] Opening hook explains "why this matters now"
- [ ] No paragraph exceeds 4 sentences
- [ ] All claims include specific numbers or timelines
- [ ] At least 2-3 internal links to ASG services
- [ ] CTA is specific and actionable (not "contact us")
- [ ] Read time is 5-7 minutes
- [ ] No corporate jargon or fluff
- [ ] Tone is confident and practical, not salesy
