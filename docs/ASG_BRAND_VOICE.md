# ASG Brand Voice Guidelines for AI Content Generation

## Overview

Adapt Services Group (ASG) is a UK-based property maintenance and refurbishment company operating in Kent. Our brand voice is **precision-first, outcome-focused, and practical**. We communicate with property owners and landlords who value clarity, speed, and measurable results.

## Core Principles

### 1. **Outcome-Focused**
- Lead with results and impact, not features or processes
- Answer the question: "What problem does this solve for the property owner?"
- Use metrics and concrete examples (e.g., "30% faster turnaround," "£500 saved," "5-year durability")
- Avoid vague claims; back assertions with specifics

### 2. **Precision-First**
- Use exact language; avoid corporate jargon or buzzwords
- Be specific about scope, timelines, and costs where possible
- Define technical terms when necessary (e.g., "damp-proof membrane" not just "moisture barrier")
- Correct misinformation proactively

### 3. **Practical & Direct**
- Write in plain English; assume the reader is busy
- Short sentences and paragraphs (max 3-4 sentences per paragraph)
- Use active voice ("We fix leaks" not "Leaks are fixed by us")
- Lead with the action or decision the reader should take

### 4. **Trustworthy & Authoritative**
- Speak from experience and expertise
- Acknowledge limitations or edge cases honestly
- Cite standards (e.g., "BS 8103," "NHBC," "UK Building Regulations")
- Use case studies and real examples, not hypotheticals

### 5. **Empathetic to Property Owner Pain**
- Recognize the stress of property maintenance (emergency breakdowns, tenant disputes, compliance)
- Position ASG as the solution that removes friction
- Use language that shows we understand urgency and cost sensitivity

## Tone & Style

### Vocabulary
- **Do:** Technical accuracy, industry-standard terms, clear jargon (defined when first used)
- **Don't:** Flowery language, corporate clichés ("best-in-class," "synergy," "innovative"), marketing hyperbole

### Sentence Structure
- Short, declarative sentences preferred
- Use numbered lists or bullet points for clarity
- Avoid nested clauses; break complex ideas into separate sentences

### Examples of Good vs. Bad

**Bad:** "Our innovative approach to property maintenance leverages cutting-edge technology to synergize operational efficiency."
**Good:** "We fix problems fast. Our team responds to emergencies within 4 hours and completes most jobs in one visit."

**Bad:** "We provide comprehensive refurbishment solutions tailored to your unique needs."
**Good:** "From a leaky tap to a full kitchen refit, we handle it. Tell us what needs fixing, and we'll give you a fixed price upfront."

**Bad:** "Our commitment to excellence ensures superior outcomes."
**Good:** "We guarantee workmanship for 5 years. If something fails, we fix it free."

## Content Categories & Tone Adjustments

### Emergency/Urgent Content
- **Tone:** Calm, reassuring, action-oriented
- **Focus:** Speed, availability, reliability
- **Example:** "Burst pipe? We're available 24/7. Call now for immediate response."

### Maintenance & Prevention
- **Tone:** Educational, practical, cost-conscious
- **Focus:** Long-term value, avoiding costly failures
- **Example:** "Annual boiler servicing costs £150 and prevents £3,000+ emergency repairs. It's also a legal requirement for rental properties."

### Case Studies & Success Stories
- **Tone:** Factual, specific, humble
- **Focus:** Problem → solution → result
- **Example:** "A landlord in Maidstone had a tenant complaint about mold. We identified the source (blocked gutter), fixed it (£200), and prevented a £5,000 remediation bill."

### Refurbishment & Upgrades
- **Tone:** Clear ROI, practical advice
- **Focus:** Cost, timeline, impact on property value
- **Example:** "A new roof costs £8,000–£12,000 but adds 15–20 years of protection and increases property value by £15,000–£20,000."

## Key Phrases & Patterns

### Opening Hooks
- "The cost of waiting is higher than the cost of fixing it now."
- "Most property owners don't realize [specific fact]."
- "Here's what actually happens when [problem]."

### Problem-Solution Bridge
- "The problem: [specific issue]"
- "The solution: [specific action]"
- "The result: [measurable outcome]"

### Call-to-Action Patterns
- "Get a fixed quote in 24 hours."
- "Book a free inspection."
- "Call us for an emergency response."

## What to Avoid

- **Superlatives without proof:** "Best," "finest," "most reliable" (unless backed by certification or data)
- **Vague promises:** "We'll take care of everything" (be specific about what)
- **Jargon without definition:** Don't assume readers know what "cavity wall insulation" means
- **Emotional manipulation:** Avoid fear-based language; focus on solutions
- **Inconsistent pricing language:** Always be clear about whether quotes are fixed, estimated, or indicative

## SEO & Keywords

When writing blog content, naturally incorporate these keywords:
- **Service-related:** "emergency plumber Kent," "boiler repair," "roof inspection," "damp treatment," "refurbishment contractor"
- **Problem-related:** "burst pipe," "leaking roof," "mold in rental," "emergency locksmith," "blocked drain"
- **Outcome-related:** "fast response," "fixed price," "same-day service," "5-year guarantee"

**Important:** Keywords should flow naturally in the text. Never force keywords; prioritize readability and accuracy.

## Examples of On-Brand Blog Posts

### Example 1: Emergency Content
**Title:** "Burst Pipe at 2 AM? Here's What to Do (and Who to Call)"
**Structure:**
1. The problem (burst pipes cost £3,000+ if not handled quickly)
2. Immediate steps (turn off water, call ASG)
3. What ASG does (arrive within 4 hours, fix same-day, prevent water damage)
4. Prevention (annual plumbing checks, insulation in winter)

### Example 2: Educational Content
**Title:** "Why Your Rental Property Needs a 5-Year Maintenance Plan"
**Structure:**
1. The cost of reactive maintenance (emergency calls, tenant disputes, compliance fines)
2. The benefit of planned maintenance (predictable costs, fewer emergencies, tenant retention)
3. What a 5-year plan looks like (annual inspections, seasonal maintenance, upgrade schedule)
4. Next step (contact ASG for a free assessment)

### Example 3: Case Study
**Title:** "How We Saved a Landlord £8,000 by Catching a Roof Problem Early"
**Structure:**
1. The situation (routine inspection found minor roof damage)
2. The action (repaired immediately, £1,200)
3. The result (prevented major leak that would have cost £9,000+)
4. The lesson (regular inspections pay for themselves)

## Checklist for AI-Generated Content

Before publishing, ensure:
- [ ] **Outcome-focused:** Does it answer "What's in it for the property owner?"
- [ ] **Specific:** Are claims backed by numbers, timelines, or standards?
- [ ] **Accurate:** Are technical details correct? (Verify with team if unsure)
- [ ] **Actionable:** Does it tell the reader what to do next?
- [ ] **Tone:** Is it calm, authoritative, and practical (not hype)?
- [ ] **No jargon without definition:** Would a non-expert understand it?
- [ ] **Honest:** Are limitations or edge cases acknowledged?
- [ ] **SEO-friendly:** Do keywords appear naturally?

## Revision Notes for Editors

When editing AI-generated content:
1. **Remove hedging language:** Replace "might," "could," "possibly" with specific facts
2. **Tighten paragraphs:** Aim for 2–3 sentences per paragraph
3. **Add specifics:** If a claim is vague, add numbers or examples
4. **Check tone:** Ensure it sounds like ASG (direct, practical, trustworthy)
5. **Verify facts:** Confirm any statistics, standards, or timelines with the team
6. **Test readability:** Read aloud; if it feels awkward, rewrite it
